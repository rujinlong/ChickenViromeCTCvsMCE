Key Findings

Uncovering the dynamics of phage communities and their responses to dietary interventions

Spatiotemporal Heterogeneity

Phage communities show distinct patterns across intestinal segments and developmental stages, with the most dramatic changes occurring between day 1 and day 7 of age.

Antibiotic Impact

Chlortetracycline (CTC) supplementation reduces phage community evenness and modulates specific phage populations, potentially through selection pressure mechanisms.

Phytogenic Alternative

Macleaya cordata extract (MCE) promotes richer and more diverse phage communities, supporting gut ecosystem homeostasis as a sustainable alternative to antibiotics.

Methodology

Advanced metagenomic approaches for comprehensive virome analysis

01

Sample Collection

360 samples from two chicken breeds (AA broilers and LY chickens) across multiple age points and dietary treatments.

02

Metagenomic Sequencing

Comprehensive viral profiling using advanced bioinformatics pipeline for identification and classification of viral contigs.

03

Dietary Interventions

Comparison of antibiotic (CTC) and phytogenic (MCE) supplementation effects on phage community structure and diversity.

04

Statistical Analysis

Advanced diversity metrics and community analysis to reveal spatiotemporal patterns and treatment effects.
